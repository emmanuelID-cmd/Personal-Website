About

Birth and Social Development

Growing up and moving through different environments exposed me to people from many backgrounds, personalities, and perspectives. Each new experience challenged me to adapt, communicate effectively, and build meaningful relationships with people from all walks of life.

Learning how to navigate those changes strengthened my ability to collaborate, solve problems, and remain confident in unfamiliar situations. Those experiences became the foundation for how I approach teamwork, leadership, and professional relationships today.

As my curiosity grew, so did my interest in technology. What began as learning everyday computer applications expanded into exploring how technology could solve problems, improve efficiency, and create new opportunities. During high school, I was introduced to tools such as Microsoft Office, Oracle, Visual Basic, Perl, and Java, which gave me my first exposure to programming and technical problem-solving.

That early foundation sparked a lasting interest in learning how technology works and how it can be used to build practical solutions. It established the mindset of continuous learning and adaptability that would later shape my academic path, professional career, and ultimately my transition into artificial intelligence and becoming a Builder.
